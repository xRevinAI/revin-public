Engineering Dependency Chain
The ordering problem: Most agreement-layer requirements have hard dependencies on two foundational items — the Pricing Library (R1) and the Agreement-Child Architecture (R2). These aren't features; they're platform investments. Without them, R3, R7, R8, R9, and the full RMM billing config stack are either blocked or brittle.

Engineering Recommendation — What to Push Into A3
🔴 Push into A3 Now
  • R1 — Pricing Library XL effort but a blocker for R9, R8. Can't close the agreements gap without it. Start the epic.
  • R2 — Agreement-Child Architecture Platform structural work. Migrate existing data under a compatibility shim. Define the data model in A3 even if full migration lands in A4.
  • R5 — Evergreen Agreements Small effort, high MSP signal. Quick win. Add it.
  • R6 — Term vs. Expiration Fields Same — schema + UI change. Minimal risk. Delivers renewal intelligence foundation.
  • RMM R-2 — Asset Type→Product Mapping Must be built before per-agreement RMM billing config. Should accompany NinjaOne integration work.
🟡 Scope for A3 if Capacity Exists
  • Gap 4 — Unblock Quote Management Currently On Hold. Unblocking it + scoping ad-hoc line items + auto-catalog write-back is M effort. Breaks the Word/Excel escape hatch.
  • RMM R-7 — Reconciliation Report High business value, M effort once Managed Assets is in. Kills the VLOOKUP workflow.
  • RMM R-3 — Agreement Asset Billing Config Depends on R-2 mapping table. If R-2 ships in A3, R-3 can follow close behind.
  • R7 — Cancellation Workflows M effort post-R2. High MSP satisfaction impact. Clean cancellation is a trust signal.
🟢 A4 — After Foundations Land
  • R8 — Inline Agreement Profitability Blocked on R1 + R2 + timesheet pipeline. A4 is the right target once the platform is ready. This is the differentiator feature — worth doing right.
  • Gap 3 — Full Configurations The 1-week A3 estimate should be a spike only. Full ConnectWise-style config management (hierarchies, dependencies) is A4 scope.
  • CRM + Distributor Integrations None in A3. Once the RMM adapter framework is proven, the pattern extends. CRM and distributor integrations follow the same playbook. A4 backlog candidates.
  • RMM R-6 — Ticket Rollup Downstream of agreement-child arch and profitability model. A4 target.
Engineering Risk Flags
⚠ Scope Underestimate

Configurations (Gap 3) has a 1-week estimate in A3. Full ConnectWise-style config management — hierarchies, dependencies, agreement linking — is a data model change and months of work. The A3 item should be scoped as a discovery spike, not delivery.

⚠ Duplicate Items

Prepaid Support Hour Billing appears twice in A3 (with and without "Agreements -" prefix). Risk of parallel implementation tracks or split ownership. Needs consolidation and single DRI before work begins.

⚠ Telecom Regression

R10 (Scale + Telecom Preservation) is a constraint, not a feature. Every agreement architecture change (R1, R2) must be tested against existing telecom billing paths. This needs formal regression coverage in CI — not a post-merge afterthought.

⚠ RMM Framework Extensibility

NinjaOne-first is fine; NinjaOne-only is a trap. If the integration is built as a point solution rather than with a vendor-agnostic adapter layer, every new RMM (Datto, N-able, Kaseya) is a partial rewrite. Architecture review required before NinjaOne dev begins.

⚠ Blocked by Foundations

13 requirements are missing from A3. Of those, 8 are downstream of R1 (pricing library) or R2 (agreement-child arch). If those two foundational items slip, the entire MSP agreement surface slides to A4+. They need early epic kickoff, not late-cycle scope add.

⚠ Quote Management On Hold

Quote Management is On Hold in A3. The quoting gap (non-catalog products, auto-catalog write-back) breaks the quote→job→invoice workflow for MSP reps. On Hold status without a stated reason or owner creates invisible risk — someone needs to revisit the hold decision.

Bottom Line
The honest engineering take: A3 is making real progress on integrations (IT Glue, Hudu, NinjaOne) and prepaid hours. But the agreement management overhaul — the thing that makes or breaks MSP deals — is almost entirely unscoped. The Pricing Library and Agreement-Child Architecture aren't features you can add later; they're the foundation everything else sits on. If we want A3 to be a credible MSP release, those two epics need to start now. If they're truly A4 work, the roadmap narrative needs to reflect that honestly — because the requirements deck doesn't.
Quick wins available: R5 (Evergreen Agreements) and R6 (Term/Expiration fields) are small-effort, high-signal additions that can ship in A3 with minimal risk. RMM R-7 (Reconciliation Report) is M effort once Managed Assets lands. These three items alone would meaningfully strengthen the MSP story for A3 without requiring foundational architecture work.
